Case: figcaption

The HTML specification states that the contexts in which the <figcaption> element can be used are:

"As the first or last child of a figure element."

"Null case" example

In this case there is a just paragraph of text and a separate paragraph containing two images, one with alt="one", one with alt="", and one without an alt attribute.

A null case example

This is to determine how a given browser "normally" treats the images, when they are not part of a figure, for comparison.

Currently conformant figure: figcaption as the first child

A figure has a figcaption as the first child, then a separate paragraph containing two images, one with alt="one", one with alt="", and one without an alt attribute.

Browsers should give "a compliant example" as the textual alternative for the figure.

Another currently conformant figure: figcaption as the last child

A figure has a paragraph containing two images, one with alt="one", one with alt="", and one without an alt attribute, then a figcaption as the last child.

Browsers should give "a compliant example" as the textual alternative for the figure.
